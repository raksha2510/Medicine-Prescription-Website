What is Depression?


Depression (major depressive disorder) is a common and serious medical illness that negatively affects how someone feels,thinks or acts. Fortunately, it is treatable. Depression causes feelings of sadness or a loss of interest even in activities you once enjoyed. It can lead to a various emotional and physical problems and can decrease your ability to function at work and at home.

What are the prescribed medicines for Depression?

1.Selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors:Doctors often start by prescribing an SSRI. These antidepressants generally have less side effects and are less likely to cause problems at higher therapeutic doses than other types of antidepressants.
2.Serotonin and norepinephrine reuptake inhibitors: Serotonin and norepinephrine reuptake inhibitors are a class of medications that are effective in treating depression.
3.Atypical antidepressants:These antidepressants are called atypical because they are different from any of the other antidepressant categories. Most-commonly prescribed antidepressants in this category include trazodone, mirtazapine (Remeron).
4.Tricyclic antidepressants: Tricyclic antidepressants such as imipramine, nortriptyline (Pamelor), amitriptyline, doxepin and desipramine (Norpramin) tend to cause more side effects than newer antidepressants. So tricyclic antidepressants generally aren't prescribed unless you've tried other antidepressants first without improvement.
5.Monoamine oxidase inhibitors: MAOIs such as tranylcypromine (Parnate), phenelzine (Nardil) and isocarboxazid (Marplan) may be prescribed, often when other medicines haven't worked. This is because they can have serious side effects. Using an MAOI requires a strict diet because of dangerous (or even deadly) interactions with foods that contain tyramine such as certain cheeses, pickles and wines and some medicines, including pain medicines, decongestants and certain herbal supplements.
Selegiline (Emsam), an MAOI that you stick on your skin as a patch, may cause fewer side effects than other MAOIs. These medicines can't be combined with SSRIs or other medicines that increase serotonin
