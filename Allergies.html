ALLERGIES

What is Allergies?


Allergies occur when your immune system reacts to a foreign substance such as pollen,bee venom or a food that doesn't cause a reaction in most people. Your immune system produces substances known as antibodies.When you have allergies, your immune system make antibodies that identify a particular allergen as harmful,even though it isn’t. When you come into contact with the allergen, your immune system reaction can inflame your skin,airways or digestive system.

What are the symptoms of Allergies?
Allergies symptoms, which depend on the substance involved, can affect your airways and nasal passages,skin and digestive system. 1.Sneezing
2.Itching of the nose,eyes
3.Face swelling
4.Runny,stuffy nose
5.Hives
6.Cough

What causes Allergies?

Airborne allergens, such as pollen, animal dander, dust mites and mold.
Certain foods, particularly peanuts, tree nuts, wheat, soy, fish, shellfish, eggs and milk.
Insect stings, such as from a bee or wasp.
Medications, particularly penicillin or penicillin-based antibiotics.
