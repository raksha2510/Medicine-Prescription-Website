DENGUE

What is Dengue?


Dengue is a mosquito-borne viral disease that has rapidly spread in world in recent years. Dengue virus is transmitted by female mosquitoes mainly of the species Aedes aegypti and, to a lesser extent, Ae. albopictus. These mosquitoes are also vectors of chikungunya, yellow fever and Zika viruses. Dengue is widespread throughout the tropics, with local variations in risk influenced by climate parameters as well as social and environmental factors.
Dengue causes a wide spectrum of disease. This can range from subclinical disease (people may not know they are even infected) to severe flu-like symptoms in those infected. Although less common, some people develop severe dengue, which can be any number of complications associated with severe bleeding, organ impairment and/or plasma leakage. Severe dengue has a higher risk of death when not managed appropriately.

What are the symptoms of Dengue?
Headache
Muscle, bone or joint pain
Nausea
Vomiting
Pain behind the eyes
Swollen glands
Rash

What causes Dengue?

You can't get dengue fever from being in touch with an infected person. Instead, dengue fever spreads through mosquito bites.When a mosquito bites an infected person, the virus enters the mosquito. Then, when the infected mosquito bites another person, the virus enters that person's body and causes an infection.

The other main causes of dengue fever can be:-
1.You live or travel in tropical areas.
2.You have had dengue fever in the past.

What are the diagnosis and precautions to take at home?

1.Stay in air-conditioned or well-screened housing.
2.Wear protective clothing.
3.Use mosquito repellent.
4.Reduce mosquito habitat.

What are the prescribed medicines for Viral Fever?

There is no specific medicine to treat dengue.You can take paracetamol to reduce fever and for pain relief. Drink plenty of fluids. Rest as much as possible.
