HYPERTENSION

What is hypertension?


High blood pressure is a common condition that affects the body's arteries. It's also called hypertension. If you have high blood pressure, the force of the blood pushing against the artery walls is consistently too high. The heart has to work harder to pump blood.

What are the symptoms of hypertension?
1.Early morning headaches
2.Nosebleeds
3.Vision changes
4.Chest pain
5.Vomiting
6.Chest pain
7.Muscle tremors

What causes Hypertension?

Things that can increase your risk of getting hypertension:
overweight.
eat too much salt and do not eat enough fruit and vegetables.
do not do enough exercise.
drink too much alcohol or coffee (or other caffeine-based drinks)
smoking
do not get much sleep or have disturbed sleep.
over the age of 65.

What are the diagnosis and precautions to take at home?

Your health care provider may recommend that you make lifestyle changes including:
Eating a heart-healthy diet with less salt.
Getting regular physical activity.
Maintaining a healthy weight or losing weight.
Limiting alcohol.
Not smoking.
Getting 7 to 9 hours of sleep daily.

What are the prescribed medicines for Hypertension?

Angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE) inhibitors reduce blood pressure by relaxing your blood vessels.
