ACNE

What is Acne?


Acne is a common skin condition that happens when hair follicles under the skin become clogged. Sebum—oil that helps keep skin from drying out—and dead skin cells plug the pores, which leads to outbreaks of lesions, commonly called pimples or zits. Most often, the outbreaks occur on the face but can also appear on the back, chest, and shoulders.

What are the symptoms of Acne?
Whiteheads Blackheads Small red,tender bumps Large,solid,painful lumps under the skin

What causes Acne?

Excess or high production of oil in the pore. Buildup of dead skin cells in the pore. Growth of bacteria in the pore.

What are the diagnosis and precautions to take at home?

Daily Skin Care such as: Clean your skin gently with a mild, non-drying soap. It may help to use a wash with salicylic acid or benzoyl if your skin is oily and prone to acne. Wash once or twice a day, and also after exercising. Shampoo your hair daily, if it is oily. Comb or pull your hair back to keep the hair out of your face.

What are the prescribed medicines for Acne?

Usually the first choice for treating acne is a tetracycline (minocycline, doxycycline) or a macrolide (erythromycin, azithromycin). A macrolide might be an option for people who can't take tetracyclines, including pregnant women and children under 8 years old.
